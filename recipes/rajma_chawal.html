Rajma Chawal

Decription:

This is a Punjabi household favourite. Growing up in my parents home, this dish was often on the menu for our much anticipated Saturday lunch. When Rajma Chawal was prepared at our school cafeteria the news would spread like wildfire and we would rush to the lunch hall with excitement. This is one of those dishes where you can easily say "Everybody loves Rajma Chawal". Think of this as North India's 'Chili sin Carne' but one where you won't miss the meat at all. Always served with jeera rice and optionally with some yoghurt and fresh onion slices.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ⅔ cups Kidney beans, soaked
  • 2 no. Black cardamom
  • ¼ tsp Dry fenugreek seeds
  • Water as required
  • 2 Bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  1. In a pressure cooker, add kidney beans, black cardamom, dry fenugreek seeds, water as required, bay leaf, water as required and salt to taste.
  2. Cover it with the lid and cook it for 3-4 whistles.
  3. In a handi or kadai, add mustard oil, once it's hot, add bay leaf, cumin seeds, onions and sauté it for a minute.
  4. Add prepared ginger garlic paste and cook it for a minute.
  5. Add little water, a pinch of asafetida, degi red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder and sauté it well.
  6. Add tomato puree, curd, salt to taste and cook it well.
  7. Add remaining rajma water, boiled kidney beans, salt to taste and cook for a while.
  8. Finish it with ghee and transfer it to a serving bowl.
  9. Garnish it with coriander sprig and serve hot with rice.
